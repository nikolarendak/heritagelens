Microfilm Digitization Project

Recovery of Data from Damaged Microfilm in Libraries

Project Overview

Our initiative aims to address the challenge of recovering data from damaged microfilm found in libraries and archives. As these microfilms deteriorate over time, they pose a significant risk to the preservation of historical records. Our project focuses on utilizing advanced technologies to restore and digitize these invaluable resources, ensuring that they are accessible for future generations.

Project Goals

  • Assess and document the condition of damaged microfilm from various library collections
  • Implement specialized techniques to clean and repair physical microfilm before digitization
  • Apply state-of-the-art Machine Learning (ML) algorithms to:
    • Enhance image quality and readability
    • Perform Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to extract and transcribe text
    • Classify document types and structure data for easy retrieval
    • Identify and mitigate any further degradation during the digitization process
    • Create high-quality digital archives that preserve the integrity of the original records
  • Collaborate with libraries and archives to integrate the recovered data into existing digital platforms

Collaboration Opportunities

We invite libraries, archives, and technology providers to collaborate on this project. Our aim is to:

  • Partner with institutions holding damaged microfilm collections
  • Work with technology providers offering advanced image processing and restoration solutions
  • Develop best practices for microfilm recovery and digitization
